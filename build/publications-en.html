NB-MLM: Effcient Domain Adaptation of Masked Language Models for Sentiment Analysis

Artificial Intelligence Laboratory
NB-MLM: Effcient Domain Adaptation of Masked Language Models for Sentiment Analysis / Nikolay Arefyev, Dmitrii Kharchev, Artem Shelmanov // Proceedings of the 2021 Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing. 2021. PP. 9114–9124.
The authors present new approach to Efficient domain adaptation of Masked Language Models (MLM). The standard MLM objective leads to inefficiency when it is used for the adaptation step because it mostly learns to predict the most frequent words, which are not necessarily related to a final task. The authors propose a technique for more effcient adaptation that focuses on predicting words with large weights of the Naive Bayes classifier trained for the task at hand, which are likely more relevant than the most frequent words. The proposed method provides faster adaptation and better final performance for sentiment analysis compared to the standard approach.

Domestication of Artificial Intelligence: Smart Speakers and Transformation of Everyday Life

Gamification Lab
Domestication of Artificial Intelligence: Smart Speakers and Transformation of Everyday Life / Andrei Korbut // Monitoring of Public Opinion: Economic and Social Changes Journal. 2021. Vol. 1. PP. 193-216. DOI: 10.14515/monitoring.2021.1.1808 (in Russian)
This paper examines what kind of transformations occur in social practices due to the introduction of smart speakers to the domestic life. The article analyses two aspects of the ‘domestication’ of artificial intelligence when using smart speakers: (1) involvement of intelligent devices in everyday home culture and (2) forms and ways of interacting with smart devices. Relying on the existing research data and his own auto-ethnographic observations and analysis of parts of conversations with the smart speaker, the author shows that, despite the inventor’s desire to make the use of smart speakers ‘natural’, users do need to develop new forms of interaction and sociality to interact with AI.

Disciplinary Digital Self-Tracking Practices: Between Emancipation and Control

Gamification Lab
Disciplinary Digital Self-Tracking Practices: Between Emancipation and Control / А.М. Давыдова, М.А. Солянова, К. Соренсен // Мониторинг общественного мнения: экономические и социальные перемены. 2021. Выпуск 1. С. 217–240. DOI: 10.14515/monitoring.2021.1.1797
Self-tracking functions are incorporated nowadays in many digital products and devices. Despite broad penetration into various spheres of life, self-tracking itself is a contradictory phenomenon. On the one hand, it enhances self-awareness opportunities for further self-transformations; on the other hand, mobile technologies penetrate individual’s private sphere, establishing control over everyday practices. The study’s focus was ‘user-tracker’ relationship. A series of interviews with users of different digital trackers was undertaken to find the basic control mechanisms. The authors conclude that all mentioned aspects of digital self-tracking are related to how users draw the line between control and emancipation.
